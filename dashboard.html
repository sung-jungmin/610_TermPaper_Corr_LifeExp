1 · Life expectancy across the world (2015)

Choropleth of life expectancy at birth in 2015. Hover for the country value.

2 · Which indicators correlate most strongly with life expectancy?

Pairwise Pearson r between life expectancy and each of 24 health indicators in 2015, sorted by sign and magnitude. Dashed lines mark the |r| = 0.40 and 0.70 strength thresholds. Nine indicators cross the |r| ≥ 0.70 cutoff.

3 · Country-level explorer

Pick an indicator from the dropdown to see country-level scatter against life expectancy plus a LOWESS smoother. Hover any point for the country name and exact values. Indicators in the dropdown are ordered with the nine strong correlates first.

4 · Robustness — Pearson vs Spearman

Each indicator's Pearson r (linear) plotted against its Spearman ρ (rank). Points on the y = x diagonal are robust to the choice of statistic. Points well above or below the diagonal are sensitive to non-linearity — notable examples flagged with their labels.

5 · Are the strong correlations stable over time?

Pearson r between life expectancy and each strong correlate at four reference years (2000, 2010, 2015, 2019). Hover a cell for the exact coefficient. Empty cells mean the indicator is not reported in the Kaggle dump for that year.

6 · Handwashing — proxy or independent predictor?

Country-level scatter of life expectancy against basic handwashing access, colour-coded by GDP per capita (PPP). Red line: bivariate fit. Green dotted line: multivariate fit holding log GDP, urbanisation, and school life expectancy at the sample mean. The slope barely halves under controls — handwashing carries independent explanatory power beyond development proxies.
